About SSB

- About the Smart Solar Backpack Project:

The SSB project is all about harnessing the sun's power to fuel innovation! We built and tested a smart solar backpack prototype designed to generate electricity and charge your devices while you're on the go.
Our initial tests were successful, The prototype generated enough power (207.48 joules in 5 minutes) to charge a phone without the cooling system active. Implementing the innovative cooling system increased the solar panel's efficiency by 30.28%, resulting in a significant boost in energy output (270.27 joules). While the cooling system itself consumes a small amount of energy (45.51 joules), the net efficiency increase remains at a positive 8.33%.







This project prioritized functionality and user-friendliness. The prototype achieved its design goals of:

  • Generating usable power for various devices
  • Storing energy efficiently
  • Maintaining a lightweight construction for comfortable wear
We believe the SSB has the potential to revolutionize the educational experience, promoting sustainable practices and empowering students to become energy-conscious innovators!

Methodology

  • 1. The solar panel was fixed to the backpack to ensure that it can endure the studentâ€™s activities and be tightly held to the backpack.
  • 2. The lithium-ion battery charger was connected to the solar panel using connecting isolated wires to ensure safety.
  • 3. The other pins of the charger were connected to the battery poles to regulate the output voltage and wattage of the solar cell and avoid battery damage.
  • 4. The batteries were put in parallel to ensure getting the highest current intensity.
  • 5. The DC-DC boost converter was connected to the wires of the circuit to get the stored energy in the battery and step up output voltage to be greater than the input.
  • 6. After that, the whole circuit was attached well to the backpack to avoid the prototype damage.


  • 1.Connecting the Arduino Nano to the breadboard to facilitate and organize the work on the connections.
  • 2.Then, connecting the temperature sensor(DHT-22) to the Arduino board and attach it to the solar panel to measure the temperature of the solar panel.

  • 3.After that, Connecting the relay module to the Arduino board to control the activation and deactivation of the fan.

  • 4.Connecting the relay module to the battery to regulate the voltage of the lithium-ion battery supplied to the fan.

  • 5.Connecting the fan to the relay module to complete the circuit of the cooling system

  • 6.After connections were finished the code was uploaded to the Arduino board to control the temperature of the solar panel.

  • 7.As depicted in Figure 3, the code activates the fan when the temperature exceeds 35 degrees Celsius. This decision is informed by the understanding that the optimal efficiency for the solar panel lies within the temperature range of 25 to 35 degrees Celsius.

Discussion

The equation used to calculate the output energy was E = V * I * t, where E is the amount of energy in joules, V is the voltage in volts, I is the current intensity in amperes, and t is the time in seconds. The energy output was calculated twice, in the absence of the cooling system and in the presence of the cooling system.

Therefore, the energy output without the cooling system was calculated as follows: E = 3.64 V * 0.19 Amp * 300 s = 207.48 j / 300 sec.

Likewise, the energy output with the cooling system was calculated as follows: E = 4.29 V * 0.21 Amp * 300 s = 270.27 j / 300 sec.

As shown in figures 5 and 6, the energy output in the presence of the cooling system was more than the energy output in the absence of the cooling system by an average percentage of 30.28%.The voltage and current output were raised by an average percentage of 17.86% and 10.53% respectively.

The smart cooling system was programmed in C++ programming language using if conditions as shown in figure 7 to turn on the fan when the temperature of the solar panel reaches 35 degrees Celsius. The DHT-22 temperature sensor measures the temperature of the solar panel. The relay module is the part of the system that activates and deactivates the fan according to the temperature which was read by the DHT-22 sensor.


Future Research

Adding more solar panels for an increased electricity output, the solar panels can be made from a malleable material that allows for easy installation of the backpack

Adding an acrylic layer to cover the solar panels from damage and dust accumulation and the layer should be in the shape of a convex lens to maximize sunlight absorption

Replacing the lithium-ion batteries with lithium-sulfur batteries which can store more power in the same space or reduce the battery size, improving the backpackâ€™s capacity or weight.

The solar panel used is polycrystalline, a better alternative is Perovskite Solar Cells: these solar cells offer incredibly high efficiency, flexibility, and potential for lower manufacturing cost.
